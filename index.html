About

Bridging Infectious Disease Diagnostics, Pathogenesis, and Therapeutic Translation

Research in infectious disease diagnostics, particularly tuberculosis and enteric infections, forms the foundation of my scientific interests. My current work focuses on improving tuberculosis detection through molecular diagnostic approaches, including Xpert MTB/RIF Ultra and sputum-based pooling strategies, with an emphasis on scalability and diagnostic performance in resource-limited, high-burden settings.

Alongside tuberculosis research, I have worked on enteric pathogens such as Cryptosporidium spp., Entamoeba histolytica, and Giardia lamblia, which has strengthened my interest in pathogen diversity, transmission dynamics, and infection burden in vulnerable populations. This work has further directed my attention toward microbiome-related research, particularly in understanding host–pathogen interactions and immunometabolic processes that influence disease outcomes..

My long-term research goal is to advance infectious disease research by integrating improvements in diagnostic strategies with mechanistic understanding of disease biology, ultimately contributing to the development of more effective therapeutic approaches informed by molecular microbiology, pathogen dynamics, and host–response interactions.
